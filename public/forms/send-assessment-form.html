SEND Mid-Camp Assessment and Decision Record
To be completed by the Camp Coordinator or other member of the leadership team where a child's additional needs have become apparent during camp, either because they were not disclosed in advance or because they are presenting more significantly than anticipated. Keep on the child's file. Share with parent or carer as appropriate.
Child and context
What has happened, when, and why is an assessment needed now?
Assessment criteria
Consider each of the following and provide notes. Answer honestly — this record exists to support the child and protect staff, not to find a justification for a particular outcome.
1. Can the child participate in activities without requiring one-to-one staff support?
Consider whether the child is able to follow group instructions, engage with activities safely, and manage transitions without needing a dedicated adult alongside them at all times.
Notes:
2. Can the child's behaviour or care needs be managed safely within the group context?
Consider whether the child's needs are placing an undue demand on staff attention, creating risk for the child or others, or requiring a level of intervention the team cannot sustain across a full session.
Notes:
3. Can the child's wellbeing and dignity be maintained appropriately?
Consider whether the child appears distressed, whether their needs are being met in a way that preserves their dignity, and whether the camp environment is suitable for them at this time.
Notes:
4. Are the child's needs having a significant impact on the safety or experience of other participants or staff?
Consider whether other children's participation is being disrupted, whether staff are stretched beyond safe capacity, or whether the situation is creating risk for the wider group.
Notes:
Reasonable adjustments considered
Before reaching a conclusion, record any adjustments tried or considered. This is important for demonstrating a fair and thorough process was followed.

Sign off
This form should be signed by the Camp Coordinator and kept on the child's file. A copy should be shared with the parent or carer where appropriate. If the child is asked to leave camp, this form provides the written record of the process followed.
